## 3. Risk Analysis — Individual Risk Items

### RISK 1: Balance Charter Hire Obligation

#### Severity Assessment: 4 — High
Box 13(II) explicitly states balance charter hire for remaining Period of Hire is payable upon early termination. If SCT terminates, for example, on **15 March 2026**, balance hire covers 16 days (15 Mar–31 Mar) = **USD 160,000 (AED 587,600)**. If termination is earlier, exposure increases proportionally.

**Financial exposure scenarios**:

| Offhire Date | Remaining Days | Balance Hire (USD) | Balance Hire (AED) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 10 Mar 2026 | 21 days | 210,000 | 771,225 |
| 15 Mar 2026 | 16 days | 160,000 | 587,600 |
| 20 Mar 2026 | 11 days | 110,000 | 404,975 |
| 25 Mar 2026 | 6 days | 60,000 | 220,350 |

This is a material cost to the project with no offsetting operational benefit after vessel offhire.

#### Likelihood Assessment: 5 — Almost Certain
The contractual language is unambiguous. Box 13 expressly states balance hire is payable. ALS has already asserted this claim in writing. There is no contractual carve-out or negotiation room unless the parties agree bilaterally.

#### Risk Score: 20 — RED (Critical)

### RISK 2: 15-Day Notice Period Non-Compliance

#### Severity Assessment: 3 — Moderate
Box 8(ii) and Box 14 both require 15 days written notice. Failure to provide timely notice could: (a) extend the hire period until proper notice is cured, (b) expose SCT to a claim of breach, (c) give ALS grounds to invoice additional days beyond the intended offhire date.

#### Likelihood Assessment: 3 — Possible
SCT has acknowledged the notice requirement in email correspondence. The risk materializes if the AGI shutdown schedule is finalized late, leaving insufficient time for 15-day notice. SCT's message states "exact schedule later" — the clock is ticking given the 31 March 2026 end date.

**Critical timeline**: If SCT wants to redeliver before 31 March, notice must be given **no later than 16 March 2026** to terminate on 31 March. For any earlier redelivery, notice must be correspondingly earlier.

#### Risk Score: 9 — YELLOW (Medium)

**Estimated exposure**: Removal work could take 2–5 days depending on scope. At USD 10,000/day hire during reinstatement, this adds USD 20,000–50,000 beyond removal contractor costs.
